Q5- Write the sum of the order and degree of the differential equation d/dx{(dy/dx)3} = 0
Solution- The degree of the differential equation is the degree of the highest order derivative, when differential coefficients are made free from radicals and fractions sign. (65/1, 2018)(65/2, 2018)(65/3, 2018)
Given differential equation is: d/dx{(dy/dx)3} = 0
=> 3(dy/dx)3-1d/dx(dy/dx) = 0
=> 3(dy/dx)2d2y/dx2 = 0
Here, order = 2 and degree = 1
∴ Sum of the order and degree = 2 + 1 = 3
